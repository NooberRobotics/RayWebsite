Electrical

The electrical systems of the ray were designed to be modular and replaceable. To do this, we created a circuit rack that allowed us to easily remove all of our circuits. The rack was actually made of another circuit board, with female headers as the plug-in points. This rack board contained all the power rail, input, and output connections on it, and allowed us to route all of our inputs directly to the TINIAH board.

Two main kinds of circuits were used. H-bridges, to control the drivetrain (right side) and IR processing circuits. The IR processing circuits detected the IR light, performed DC blocking, filtered to the right frequency (using a biquad filter), amplified the signal, and rectified it to a near DC signal that could be taken as an analog input to the TINAH board.
